Beyond Code — How I Think About Products

Alongside my technical work, I’ve spent time looking at how products are actually discovered and experienced — especially through my work with GlassRoom, a leadership and simulation-based learning company.

Practically, that has meant helping analyse outreach experiments, looking at LinkedIn performance, and understanding how content, messaging and basic web presence influence credibility. It has also made me more aware of how small changes in UX, clarity and structure can affect whether someone engages or drops off.

This complements my development work: I don’t just write code, I try to understand the context in which it runs — who it is for, how they arrive there, and what “success” looks like for the product or feature.

What this work has taught me
